# One Health Risk & Policy Scenario Modelling (R)

This repository provides a structured framework for analysing zoonotic and biosecurity risk within a One Health context.

This repository provides a conceptual and analytical framework rather than a production-ready system.

It focuses on translating biological, environmental, and agricultural variables into a composite risk signal, and evaluating how policy interventions modify that risk.

---

## Context

Biological risk emerges from the interaction between:

* livestock systems
* wildlife interfaces
* environmental conditions
* human exposure patterns
* biosecurity and veterinary infrastructure

Effective decision-making requires structured comparison of interventions under conditions of uncertainty.

---

## Objective

This project aims to:

* aggregate multi-domain One Health indicators
* generate a composite zoonotic risk score
* evaluate the impact of policy interventions
* support structured, reproducible decision-making

---

## Model Overview

The model incorporates:

* livestock density
* wildlife contact
* water quality risk
* vaccination coverage
* veterinary access
* human exposure
* farm-level biosecurity

These variables are combined into a weighted composite risk score.

---
## How to Run

Run scripts in order:

1. scripts/01_generate_sample_data.R  
2. scripts/02_risk_scoring_model.R

## Methodological Approach

This is a deterministic, scenario-based model:

* variables are normalised
* combined into a composite score
* modified under different policy conditions
* compared across scenarios

The model is designed to prioritise interpretability and structured reasoning, supporting decision-making rather than prediction.

---

## Limitations

* uses synthetic data
* does not model transmission dynamics
* does not include stochastic variability
* does not account for temporal effects

This model should be understood as a conceptual decision-support framework.
